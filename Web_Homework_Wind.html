Summary: Lattitude vs. Wind Velocity

As the air near the surface of the earth becomes warmer, the particles becomes warmer, the the particles of the air move farther apart. The air becomes lighter and rises. The lighter air pushes down on the surface of the earth with less pressure. A low-pressure area forms. Cold air is heavier than warm air. Its matter is more closely packed together. It pushes down harder on the earth's surface than warm air does. Therefore, a cold air mass is called a high-pressure area.Air temperature varies between day and night and from season to season due to changes in the heating Earth's atmosphere. Because of the sun's warming effect, there are more winds during the day. Air masses also differ in temperature. A warm front precedes a warm air mass. Warm air is less dense than cold air, so warm air rides up and over the cold air, causing winds. Conversely, a cold front, the leading edge of a cold air mass, also creates wind.
