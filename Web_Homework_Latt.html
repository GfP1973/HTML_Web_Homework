Summary: Lattitude vs. Temperature

The purpose of the project was to analyze how weather patterns change as you get closer to the equator. Weather data was gathered for 500 cities over the course of three years using the OpenWeatherData API and results rendered graphically with MatPlotLib functionality. As is apparent from the plot of city lattitude and temperature, temperature increases and decreases in a predictable pattern as a result of the proximity to the equator at 0 deg lattitude. The relationship between temperature and distance from the equator proposed by the original hypothesis is directly supported by the data presented here. Although this conclusion is intuitive, the empirical confirmation provided serves as the foundation for the relational conclusions we will assert based on the additional measures for humidity, cloud cover, and wind velocity. Temperature is a key driver for all weather patterns, and directly influences evaporation and condensation rates, therefore directly influencing regional humidity and cloud cover. Furthermore, in the most basic terms, wind is generated by the uneven heating and cooling of the surface of the earth.
